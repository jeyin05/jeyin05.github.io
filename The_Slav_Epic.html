The Slav Epic

Mucha's lifelong aspiration was to create a masterpiece for his nation.

He saw the creation of The Slav Epic as his artistic mission.


The Slav Epic

The Slav Epic is Alphonse Mucha’s magnum opus, a colossal series of twenty monumental oil paintings created over eighteen years (1910–1928). This work represents his ultimate nationalistic mission, moving away from the decorative Art Nouveau style to a grand, historical realism. Mucha conceived the Epic as a vast pictorial narrative dedicated to the entire Slav people, chronicling their history, struggles, and hopes, from ancient origins to modern unity.


Supported by the American philanthropist Charles R. Crane, Mucha returned to Prague to execute these huge canvases, some over 8 meters wide. In 1928, the completed series was officially donated to the City of Prague, standing today as Mucha’s most profound and enduring legacy to his nation.
